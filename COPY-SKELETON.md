## The Content Flywheel — Five-Phase Arc (from #121 Research)

Source: Atom #121 (The Heart Follows the Treasure) + Schwartz + Friston + Lakoff + Warren + constitutive gravitation model.

**The mechanism:** Content doesn't create desire — it names what's already constituted inward. Each piece deposits a micro-formation into the reader's constitution, which changes what they seek next, which brings them back. The flywheel is not algorithmic. It's ontological.

| Phase | Name | What happens | Kevin's content system |
|-------|------|-------------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Deposit** | First post names what they feel but can't articulate (P1) | They save it, share it, think about it. Micro-deposit made. |
| 2 | **Constitution** | After 3-5 posts, perception shifts | They start seeing AI differently — not as tool but as thinking partner. Kevin's frame is installing. |
| 3 | **Gravitation** | They seek more — subscribe, follow, check | The heart gravitates toward the treasury. Attention routes toward Kevin's content. |
| 4 | **Identification** | They adopt the language and identity | They think of themselves as "someone who collaborates with AI, not just uses it." Kevin's vocabulary becomes theirs. |
| 5 | **Defense** | They share, advocate, push back against old frame | They've become evangelists — not because Kevin asked, but because the treasury merged with their identity. |

**Practical implication:** The first 5-10 posts must be NAMING posts (Phase 1 deposits). Don't teach, don't explain, don't share tips. Just name what people already feel. The naming IS the deposit. The heart follows.

**Why this is different from a marketing funnel:** A funnel extracts value at each stage (attention → email → sale). The flywheel DEPOSITS value at each stage (formation → deeper formation → identity shift). The monetization comes because the reader has been genuinely formed — they want the deeper version (course, community, advisory) because they've become a different person, not because they were funneled.

### First 3 Naming Posts (Phase 1 deposits — launch sequence)

These are the content that starts the flywheel. Each names what the visitor already feels but hasn't articulated:

1. **"You've saved 47 AI videos. You know why you haven't watched them."**
   Names: the guilt of knowing something matters and not acting. Universal. High save/share potential. The number "47" is specific enough to feel real (Schwartz: specificity signals authenticity).

2. **"The gap between what AI CAN do and what you GET it to do — that's the real skill."**
   Names: the frustration of having tried AI and been both amazed and disappointed. Reframes: the skill isn't prompting — it's thinking WITH AI. This is the bridge post toward Kevin's method.

3. **"School taught you to specialize. Something in you always resisted."**
   Names: the polymathic grief — being forced into one box when you wanted to be many things. AI as the redemptive gift. This post will attract Kevin's ideal reader — the person who has always felt like a misfit in specialized systems.

**Post 4-5 (after audience knows Kevin's voice):**

4. **"Tuesday, 9am: Here's exactly what AI-as-thinking-partner looks like."**
   Shows: a real day. Not tips — a documentary of the method in action. This is the "model they're missing" post.

5. **"I'm a first-time father with $20k and an AI thinking partner. Here's month one."**
   The synthesis post. Links back to 1-4. This is the one that goes wide. Building in public, vulnerability, stakes, receipts.

**The single underlying pain:** "I know AI matters. I can't figure out how to start. And every day I don't, the gap gets wider."

### Ten symptoms of that one pain:

| # | Symptom | The feeling underneath |
|---|---------|----------------------|

| 2 | Know "big thing is coming" but don't know how to change | Paralysis |

### What they're looking for:

Not a tutorial. Not a course. Not hype. **A person who's actually living it — who looks like them (busy, real life, real constraints) — showing what it actually looks like.**

Kevin IS that model. First-time father, real financial pressure, real family life. Not a tech bro. Not a 23-year-old with no responsibilities. A real person integrating AI into ALL of it — and sharing the journey.

### Why Kevin is credible to this person:

- Same constraints (family, limited funds, limited time)
- SHOWING, not just telling (real artifacts: app, dashboard, knowledge system)
- "Collaborating" language signals partner, not tool — matches their intuition
- Multi-domain proof (not just one trick — the whole life)
- Building in public = they can verify it's real in real time
